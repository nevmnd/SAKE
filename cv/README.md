# CV HOWTO

This folder contains resources to help you write and maintain a professional CV. As a base you can use a [template](md_converter/CV.md).
Beyond that, inside you can find a [simple tool](md_converter/README.md) to convert CV in markdown format to HTML and PDF.

---

## Tips for Writing a Professional CV

Here are some best practices to help you create an effective and polished resume:

### Keep it clear and concise

- Use clean, simple formatting  
For some reason many people love complex formatting and have vicious tendency to use [canva.com](https://www.canva.com/templates) templates for that. Please, avoid as it is poorly recognizable by ATS. And if you do not agree - how do you think ATS should parse you language level expressed by a scale of several dots?
- Limit your CV to 1–2 pages  
It is considered that ATS cannot parse more than that. Besides, every recruiter has 1 minute at most to check your CV, so be concise.

### Add concise professional summary

It should be very short, ideally 2-3 sentences so that recruiter could get an impression of whom he/she is dealing with just from reading this paragraph.  
Example you can find in the above mentioned [CV template](md_converter/CV.md). Yes, it is AI generated and some wording can be improved - so do your best, imagine how would you describe how awesome you are to the person who does not know anything about you / your field / your stack / whatever.

### Be specific with your experience

- **Focus on Impact (Use the XYZ Formula)**  
`Accomplished X, measured by Y, by doing Z`  
Example:  
`Increased system uptime by 80% by redesigning server architecture and implementing real-time monitoring tools.`
- **Add numbers even if you really (really!) do not want to**  
Casual developer hardly can explain how his/her work affects business. This way you show that you, actually, **do** care.
And even if you do not know which numbers to put - make them up! But also, please, make a legend for that. You can always relate to some metrics which you got from you PM for example.
- **Use action verbs**  
Start bullet points with strong verbs like:  
`Led, Developed, Implemented, Designed, Increased, Reduced, Built, Streamlined`  
But try to make it sound as not AI generated.

### Tailor It to the Role (if you have a time for that)

- Align your experience and keywords with the specific job description.
- Customize your summary and skills for each application.
- From my personal experience it is not required if your stack matches job description more or less and if your CV is self-selling.

### Never stop on version 1

You **never should stop adapting your CV**. Put it to the git and enhance it again and again each time you think that you can make it better. Try to also find some recruiters that can tell you what you can improve.
